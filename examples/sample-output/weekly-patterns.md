# Weekly Patterns — 2026-04-07 to 2026-04-13

## Productivity Pattern

| Day | Active hours | Commits | Context switches | Output score |
|-----|-------------|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| Mon | 4.5h | 6 | 5 | Medium |
| Tue | 3.2h | 4 | 8 | Low |
| Wed | 5.8h | 9 | 3 | High |
| Thu | 6.3h | 11 | 3 | High |
| Fri | 4.0h | 5 | 6 | Medium |

**Finding:** Days with <=3 context switches produced 2.1x more commits. Wednesday and Thursday were deep-work days. Tuesday had 8 switches between projects — lowest output of the week.

## Focus Distribution

- Auth system refactor: 42% of total coding time
- Frontend updates: 23%
- Code reviews: 15%
- DevOps / CI fixes: 12%
- Misc: 8%

## Recurring Behaviors

1. **Morning ramp-up:** First commit consistently appears 45-60min after first terminal command. Suggests warmup period before productive coding.

2. **Research before implementation:** On 4/5 days, opened browser tabs 10-20min before starting a new coding block. Pattern: research → code → test → commit.

3. **Late-day Slack:** Slack usage spikes after 4pm every day. Correlated with fewer commits in the last hour.

4. **Test-driven on refactors:** Ran test suite 6x on Thursday (auth refactor) vs 1x on Friday (frontend). More testing when stakes are higher.

## Weak Spots

- **Tuesday thrash:** 8 projects in one day. Three of those projects had <15min of activity — likely interruptions, not planned work.
- **PR review backlog:** 2 reviews requested this week, 0 completed. Accumulating debt.
- **TODO accumulation:** 3 new TODOs written this week, 1 resolved. Net +2 open threads.

## Suggestion

Block Tuesday mornings for deep work. Current pattern shows Tuesday as the most fragmented day consistently (3 weeks running). Even 2 hours of protected time would reduce context switches by ~40%.
